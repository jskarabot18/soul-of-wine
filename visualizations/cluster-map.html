Cluster Map

59 wine regions plotted by geography, coloured by identity cluster. Regions that share a map neighbourhood often belong to completely different identity types — and regions on opposite sides of the world can share the same soul.

What the map reveals

Geography clusters regions by proximity. Identity does not. Burgundy and Santa Cruz Mountains sit 9,000 km apart but share the same identity cluster (Old World Interior). Sicily and Napa Valley are separated by an ocean but both belong to New World Reinvention. The Barossa Valley and Santorini — antipodal in every geographic sense — are united by struggle. The map is not the soul.
